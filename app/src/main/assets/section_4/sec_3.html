4. Kidney changes in patients with DN

  • Structural changes: glomerular hypertrophy, hypertrophy of mesangial cells, thickening of the glomerular basement membrane, matrix accumulation.1
  • Functional changes:
    • Glomerular hyperfiltration: defined as glomerular filtration rate greater than 150 mL/min.1 The first changes of DN include glomerular hyperfiltration and renal hypertrophy.1
    • Microalbuminuria: corresponds to urinary albumin between 30-300 mg/day; 20-200 μg/min. or 30-300 mg/g in albumin/creatinine ratio.5
    • Macroalbuminuria: if albumin concentration > 300 mg/day; > 200 μg/min. or > 300 mg/g in albumin/creatinine ratio.5
    • Proteinuria: is concentration > 300 mg/day or > 200 μg/min. of any protein in urine.5

The cause of microalbuminuria is altered synthesis of proteoglycans of the extracellular matrix (mesangium and basement membrane) with changes in electrical membrane composition and leakage of albumin.1

Microalbuminuria is the first clinical manifestation of DN.1 It may initially be intermittent and associated with exercise; if microalbuminuria becomes persistent, it can be demonstrated by urine test. Persistent microalbuminuria is established when 2 / 3 or 3 tests are positive for microalbuminuria, within a sampling period ranging between 3 and 6 months.1

Microalbuminuria is associated with increased cardiovascular risk; the association between DN and cardiovascular risk is continuous.6 People with microalbuminuria have twice the cardiovascular risk than people with normoalbuminuria.6

Persistent proteinuria is associated with decreased GFR, hypertension, poor prognosis and progressive deterioration of renal function.1
