2. Epidemiology

Data from IDF Atlas shows that 415 million adults have diabetes in 2015. By 2040 this will rise to 642 million. The North America and Caribbean Region has the highest prevalence of diabetes compared to the other IDF regions with 12.9% of the adult population affected and the highest prevalence of impaired glucose tolerance (IGT).

One adult in eight in the North America and Caribbean Region has diabetes. A further 51.8 million people, or 15.0% of adults aged 20-79 in the North America and Caribbean Region have impaired glucose tolerance, putting them at high risk of developing type 2 diabetes. By 2040 it is estimated that 60.5 million adults will be living with diabetes and a further 67.4 million will have impaired glucose tolerance.2

Type 2 diabetes mellitus is the leading cause of end-stage renal disease (ESRD) worldwide.5

In the United States, 43% of patients with history of DM have microalbuminuria and 8% have macroalbuminuria at the time of diagnosis.6

The duration of diabetes is directly related to the presence of DN.1

In patients with type 1 DM, the cumulative incidence of DN at age 40 is 45% to 50%.1 In patients with type 2 diabetes, it is between 39% and 50%.1
