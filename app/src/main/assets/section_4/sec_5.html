6. Diagnosis and Follow-up of DN

All patients with DM should be screened for DN.16

In patients with type 1 diabetes mellitus, screening should begin five years after the diagnosis of diabetes.6

In patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus, screening should begin at the time of diagnosis of diabetes.6

Screening should include:

  • Measurement of the albumin/creatinine ratio in a urine sample (Table 3).6 It is considered positive if there are two of three positive samples during three to six months.6,12
    Microalbuminuria should not be tested if urinary tract infection, presence of hematuria, acute fever, strenuous exercise, pronounced acute hyperglycemia, uncontrolled hypertension or heart failure.14
  • Creatinine measurement and estimation of glomerular filtration rate.6 Estimation of GFR can be done using the Cockcroft-Gault and the full or abbreviated Modified of Diet in Renal Disease (MDRD) equations (Table 4).12,14 The reference range of GFR in younger individuals is 80 to 130 mL/min./1.73m2 It decreases in average 10 mL/min. per decade of life, after age 50.14

In all patients with Type 2 DM the following should be evaluated:

  • Measure blood pressure13
  • Estimate glycemic control13
  • Estimate lipid control13
  • Whether the patient smokes13
  • Assess for the presence of retinopathy and neuropathy13
  • Determine the presence of cardiovascular disease13

In the following circumstances, alternative causes of kidney disease must be considered:

  • Microalbuminuria in the absence of retinopathy6
  • Rapid decrease in GFR6
  • Rapid increase of microalbuminuria or presence of nephrotic syndrome
  • Refractory hypertension6
  • Presence of active urinary sediment6
  • Signs or symptoms of another pathology6
  • >30% reduction in GFR after 2-3 months of starting an inhibitor of the angiotensinaldosterone system6
