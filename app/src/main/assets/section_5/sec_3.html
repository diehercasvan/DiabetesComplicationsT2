4. How and why diabetic foot occurs?

Foot ulcers in diabetic patients occur by an interaction between risk factors and minor trauma.9
In two thirds of patients with foot ulcers, the presence of the triad (neuropathy, deformity and trauma) can be observed.11
Most ulcers in diabetic patients are considered neuropathic.9,11,12
Among patients with foot ulcers: 60% have dominant peripheral neuropathy; 20%, peripheral vascular disease; and the remaining 20%, a combination of both.13
