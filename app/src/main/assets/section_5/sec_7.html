8. Ulcer assessment in diabetic patients

According to the etiology, 60% of ulcers are neuropathic; 20%, vascular, and 20%, neuroischemic.19
According to evolution, ulcers can be acute if the ulcer heals within 2 weeks; chronified if lasts for 2-8 weeks; and chronic if heals within 8-10 weeks.20

From a pathophysiological point of view, an acute ulcer has metabolically active cells, good levels of growth factors, low levels of proteases and normal progression of the inflammatory phase.20 In chronic ulcers, there are inactive cells, high levels of proteases, low levels of growth factors and persistence of the inflammatory state.20

Neuropathic ulcers are located at the base of the toes and metatarsal heads; ischemic ulcers involve the heel, the dorsum, the tip of the toes and the lateral edge of the foot.21

When evaluating an ulcer, the following should be assessed: type of ulceration (neuropathic, ischemic, neuroischemic, venous, etc.); time of evolution of the ulceration; location; size (length, width and depth); the depth of the ulceration (clean, infected, granulation tissue); ulcer borders and surrounding skin; if there is infection (pain, secretion, edema, erythema, odor, necrosis, etc.); signs of epithelization or neovascularization of the ulceration; and classify the ulcer (Table 6 and 7).22
