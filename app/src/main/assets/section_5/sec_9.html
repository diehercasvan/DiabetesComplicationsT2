10. Preventive treatment of diabetic foot

Prevention is the mainstay of treatment of diabetic foot.14,19The treatment should always be individualized, specific and prophylactic.14

Prevention of foot lesions in diabetic patients should be based on:

  • Optimal metabolic control.
  • Regular evaluation of the feet and footwear: the periodicity of the examination should be determined according to the risk classification for diabetic foot (Table 9).24
  • Detection and intervention of risk factors for the development of diabetic foot24
  • Prescription of proper footwear24
    • Protective measures are indicated when there are: biomechanical abnormalities of the foot and plantar pads, presence of hyperkeratosis, reduced sensitivity and impaired skin by ischemia.25
    • The corrective measures are indicated in patients with impaired support and drop of forefoot, and include: the metatarsal band, the metatarsal pad, silicones, insoles and retrocapital bar.25
  • Correction of deformities and other non-ulcerative foot pathologies24
  • Education of patients and families about foot care: education has been shown to reduce the risk of ulceration and amputation in patients with diabetes mellitus.24

It is essential that health professionals are educated to make a proper assessment of the feet in patients with diabetes mellitus.24
