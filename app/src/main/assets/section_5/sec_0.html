1. Definition

According to the diabetic foot research group of the School of Medicine of the National University of Colombia, diabetic foot is: The peripheral vascular or neurological disturbances, of varying degrees, that occur in diabetic patient feet, causing one or more of the following complications: ulceration, infection or deep tissue destruction, associated with joint, bone and skin damage, which may lead to amputation, with consequent functional, psychological and economic impact for patients, their families and the health system.1

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), diabetic foot syndrome is: “foot ulceration, infection and/or gangrene associated with diabetic neuropathy and different degrees of peripheral artery disease; It results from complex interactions between several factors, mainly metabolic”.2
