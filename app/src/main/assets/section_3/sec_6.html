7. Treatment for acute stroke

  • General measures
    • Airway management: intubation and mechanical ventilation if necessary.19
    • Temperature: control hypothermia and hyperthermia.19
    • Blood pressure: control must be strict, avoiding sudden drop.17 Slow reduction of blood pressure (10%-20% during the first 24 hours) is recommended.19
    • Avoid hypotension administering iso-osmolar fluids.19
  • Control of blood glucose. Hyperglycemia is present in 20% to 50% of patients with ischemic stroke; It affects to 100% of diabetic patients and 50% of non-diabetics.18 Hyperglycemia is associated with a worse prognosis and higher rates of cerebral hemorrhages.18 Hypoglycemia is also associated with a worse prognosis.19 The management of these conditions should be immediate and aggressive.19
  • Reperfusion therapy. The use of thrombolysis with alteplase (rt-PA) is recommended for patients with ischemic stroke within the first 3 hours of onset of symptoms and who have none of the exclusion criteria for this therapy.19 It is considered a safe and effective treatment that has shown to improve survival and function of patients with ischemic stroke.19
  • Anticoagulation therapy. It is recommended for deep venous thrombosis (DVT) prophylaxis in immobilized patients.19
  • Antiplatelet therapy. Administration of acetylsalicylic acid (or another antiplatelet agent in case of contraindication for ASA) is recommended for prophylaxis in patients who will not undergo thrombolysis.19
