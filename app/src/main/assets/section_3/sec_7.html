8. Prevention of stroke

The following are the recommendations of the American Heart Association and the American Stroke Association (2011) for the primary prevention of stroke in diabetic patients:

  • In patients with diabetes the BP goal is <130/80 mm Hg (Class I; Level of Evidence A).14
  • Treatment of hypertension in adults with diabetes with an ACEI or an ARB is useful (Class I; Level of Evidence A).14
  • Treatment of adults with diabetes with a statin, especially those with additional risk factors, is recommended (Class I; Level of Evidence A).14
  • Treatment with an statin medication in addition to therapeutic lifestyle changes with LDL-cholesterol goals, is recommended for primary prevention of ischemic stroke in patients with diabetes (Class I; Level of Evidence A).14
  • The use of monotherapy with a fibrate to lower stroke risk might be considered for patients with diabetes (Class IIb; Level of Evidence B).14
  • The benefit of acetylsalicylic acid (ASA) for reduction of stroke risk has not been satisfactorily demonstrated for patients with diabetes; however, administration of ASA may be reasonable in those at high CVD risk (Class IIb; Level of Evidence B).14
