7. Treatment

7.1. Recommendations for coronary artery disease prevention in patients with type 2 diabetes:

  • All patients with diabetes should:
    • Make therapeutic changes in lifestyle9
    • Loose weight9
    • Increase physical activity (30 minutes of moderate physical activity, 3-5 times per week)9
    • Moderate consumption of alcohol9
    • Discontinue smoking99
    (Recommendation A)
  • All patients with diabetes should achieve and maintain the proposed goals for blood pressure, lipids, blood glucose and glycosylated hemoglobin (Recommendation AA). See Table 2.9
  • All patients with diabetes and coronary artery disease or a cardiovascular risk factor should receive acetylsalicylic acid in doses between 75 mg and 150 mg per day (Recommendation A).9
  • All patients over 55 years with an additional risk factor (hypertension, microalbuminuria, dyslipidemia or smoking) should receive an ACE inhibitor. (Recommendation A).9

7.2. Recommendations for the treatment of patients with diabetes after a coronary event

  • All patients with diabetes should:
    • Make therapeutic changes in lifestyle9
    • Reduce weight9
    • Increase physical activity (30 minutes of moderate exercise, 3-5 times per week)9
    • Moderate alcohol consumption9
    • Stop smoking9 (Recommendation A)
    • All patients with diabetes and coronary artery disease should be assessed by a cardiologist to establish the degree of coronary arteries involvement and determine the course of treatment (Recommendation D).9
    • All patients with diabetes and coronary artery disease should receive acetylsalicylic acid in doses between 75 mg and 100 mg (or 150 mg) per day (Recommendation AA).9
    • All patients with diabetes and coronary artery disease should be treated with a statin (Recommendation AA).9
    • All patients with diabetes and MI should be treated with a beta blocker, unless contraindicated (Recommendation B).9
    • All patients with diabetes who have suffered a coronary event should be treated with an ACE inhibitor, especially during the first 24 hours (Recommendation AA).9
    • All patients with diabetes and MI should be considered for thrombolytic therapy similarly than patients without diabetes (Recommendation A).8
    • In patients with diabetes and acute coronary syndrome, adenosine diphosphate (ADP) receptor inhibitors may be indicated in combination with acetylsalicylic acid (Recommendation C).8
    • In patients with diabetes, treatment for revascularization should include coronary artery bypass surgery rather than percutaneous revascularization (Recommendation A).8
    • Mechanical reperfusion by percutaneous coronary intervention is the treatment of choice for revascularization in diabetic patients with MI (Recommendation A).8
    • All patients with diabetes and post-infarction coronary artery disease should engage in a cardiac rehabilitation program (Recommendation D).9
