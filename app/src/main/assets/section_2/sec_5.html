6. Diagnosis

The Latin American Diabetes Association (ALAD) recommends for the diagnosis of coronary disease in diabetic patients:

  • An ECG is the initial study indicated for all people over age 30 with diabetes (Recommendation D).9
  • The stress test is recommended for the study of all patients with diabetes suffering from typical or atypical signs of angina or possible or probable signs of coronary heart disease (Recommendation A).9
  • In all diabetic patients over 40 years with one or more risk factors for coronary heart disease (microalbuminuria, clinical nephropathy, peripheral vascular disease or autonomic neuropathy), a stress test must be performed (Recommendation B).9
  • If initial stress test is normal, repeating the test within 2 to 5 years after the initial test is recommended (Recommendation C).9
  • In people who can not perform a stress test (very obese or with limitations) evaluation using nuclear medicine (scintigraphy) or stress echocardiogram is recommended (Recommendation D).9
