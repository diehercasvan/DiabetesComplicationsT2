1. DEFINITION

Painful diabetic neuropathy (PDN) is defined as the presence of symptoms of impaired peripheral nervous system (PNS) function in diabetic patients, after excluding other possible causes.1

Neuropathic pain is originated as a direct consequence of injury or disease affecting somatosensory system.2

Anatomically, neuropathic pain can be of peripheral origin (if peripheral nervous system is affected), central origin (if central nervous system is involved), and sympathetic origin (if autonomic nervous system is affected).3
