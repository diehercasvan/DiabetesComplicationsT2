7. Treatment for painful diabetic neuropathy

The most cost-effective measure to prevent the onset and slow the progression of neuropathy in patients with diabetes mellitus is adequate glycemic control.1

Recommendations of the American Academy of Neurology (in conjunction with the American Association of Neuromuscular and Electrodiagnostic Medicine and the American Academy of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation-2011) for the treatment of painful diabetic neuropathy, based on evidence.

  1. Anticonvulsants:
    • Pregabalin should be indicated as a treatment for PDN (Level A).7
    • The gabapentin and sodium valproate may be considered for treatment of PDN (Level B).7
    • There is insufficient evidence to support or refute the use of topiramate for the treatment of PDN (Level U).7
    • Oxcarbazepine, lamotrigine and lacosamide should probably not be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level B).7
  2. Antidepressants:
    • Amitriptyline, venlafaxine, and duloxetine should be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level B). Data are insufficient to recommend one of these agents over the others.7
    • Venlafaxine may be added to gabapentin for a better response (Level C).7
    • There is insufficient evidence to support or refute the use of desipramine, imipramine, fluoxetine, or the combination of nortriptyline and fluphenazine for the treatment of PDN (Level U).7
  3. Opiods:
    • Dextromethorphan, morphine, tramadol, and oxycodone should be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level B).7 Data are insufficient to recommend one agent over the other.7
  4. Other treatments:
    • Capsaicin and isosorbide dinitrate (spray) should be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level B).7
    • Lidocaine patch may be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level C).7
    • Clonidine, pentoxifylline, and mexiletine should probably not be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level B).7
    • There is insufficient evidence to support or refute the use of alpha lipoic acid in the treatment of PDN (Level U).7
  5. Non-pharmacological measures:
    • Percutaneous electrical nerve stimulation should be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level B).7
    • Electromagnetic field treatment, low-intensity laser treatment, and Reiki therapy should probably not be considered for the treatment of PDN (Level B).7
