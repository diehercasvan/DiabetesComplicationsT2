b. Additional tests for patients with neuropathic pain
For a comprehensive approach to patients with neuropathic pain, additional pain assessment tests should be included, using the visual analogue scale, the assessment of health status (eg. SF-12 questionnaire), the evaluation of disability (eg. Oswestry disability index - ODI), the presence of anxiety or depression (eg. Hospital Anxiety and Depression Scale - HADS), and the evaluation of quality of patient’s sleep (eg. Medical Outcomes Study - MOS).16

c. Physical examination
Neurological examination of patients with painful diabetic neuropathy should aim to assess the somatosensory, motor and autonomic function of the patient (Table 5).1 The physical examination should assess the painful, thermal, and vibratory sensations, as well as proprioception, reflexes, muscle strength, gait, muscle trophism, autonomic function (sweating, blood pressure, heart rate, etc.).1,9

A score of 7/46 is considered positive for diabetic neuropathy.18
The sensitivity and specificity achieved by this test are 80% and 100% respectively for diabetic neuropathy diagnosis.18

Other tests used in special conditions include: EMG, somatosensory evoked potentials, test for pain thermal sensitivity caused by thermal stimulation measured by thermotest, pain sensitivity measured by algometer, and laser-evoked cortical potentials (LEP).1
