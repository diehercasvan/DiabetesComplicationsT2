2. Epidemiology

According to the International Diabetes Federation (IDF), in 2013, 8.3% of adults worldwide (i.e. 382 million people) had diabetes. Without appropriate interventions, this number will be increased to over 592 million people in 2035, which represents an increase of 55%.4

In Mexico, the diabetes overall prevalence in adults is 14.4%, and diabetes is the leading cause of mortality among men and women all over the country.5

Diabetic neuropathy is the most common symptomatic chronic complication among diabetic patients.1

Diabetic neuropathy prevalence ranges from 7%, within the first year after diagnosis, to 50% in patients who have had diabetes for 25 years.6 If patients with subclinical neuropathic disturbances are included, prevalence may exceed 90%.6

Some reviews show that between 16% and 25% of diabetic patients (being treated with insulin and/or oral hypoglycemic agents) have PDN.1,7

This condition produces a specific type of neuropathic pain, leading to suffering, marked impairment of quality of life, and disability.1

As other types of pain, pain of PDN affects both physical and emotional aspects.1 These emotional and affective disturbances may lead to feelings of frustration, anxiety and depression, which in turn alter pain perception. All these complex interactions make treatment difficult.1

Up to 12.5% of cases of PDN are not reported, and up to 39% remain untreated.7
